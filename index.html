The Master of Puppets

  • 1981 - Lars Ulrich advertised in a LA newspaper, The Recylcer, posting "Drummer looking fo other metal musicians to jam with Tygers of Pan Tang, Diamon Head, and Iron Maiden."
  • Late 1981 - James Hetfield joins Ulrich to sing and play guitar based on a record deal Ulrich arranged to record a single for an upcoming album, Metal Massacre.
  • 1982 - Metallica releases their first single "Hit the Lights"
  • 1983 - Blown away by bassist, Cliff Burton's, ability to shred, the band extends offer to join Metallica. Cliff agreed on the condition the band move to San Francisco. The addition rounds out what most fans would call the founding members: Lars Ulrich (drums), James Hetfield (vocal/guitar), Dave Mustaine (lead guitar), and Cliff Burton (bass).
  • Mid 1983 - Metallica agrees to produce its first record "Kill 'Em All" with Megaforce Records in New York City. On the route, the band decided to remove Mustaine from the band due to drug and alcohol abuse. On the same afternoon Kirk Hammett replaces.
  • 1984 - Metallica releases its second studio album "Ride the Lightning" which reached No 100 on the Billboard 200.
  • 1985 - the band performs its largest venue to date alongside Bon Jovi and Ratt to 70,000 people.
  • 1986 - Release their third album "Master of Puppets" which is often referred to as the greatest metal album of all time.
  • Mid 1986 - On a bus tour in Sweden, the band members drew cards to determine which bunks they would sleep. The bus driver lost control, flipping the bus several times. Tragically, an unwounded Ulrich, Hammet, and Hetfield discover Cliff dead pinned under the bus.
  • Late 1986 - With Burton's family blessings, the band hired Jason Newsted as their new bassist and backup vocalist.
  • 1988 - Still reeling from Cliff's death, the band releases their fourth album "...And Justice for All" of thrashing lyrics, guitar, and drums regrettfully minimizing Jason's contribution on bass.
  • 1989 - Nominated for the new "Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance Vocal or Instrument" award at the Grammy Awards. As the favorites, the band iconically lost in one of the Grammy's biggest blunders to folk artist, Jethro Toll.
  • 1990 - Riding off the success of "...And Justic For All", the band was looking to break into the mainstream with the hire of Aerosmith, Bon Jovi, and Mötley Crüe producer, Bob Rock
  • 1991 - The release of their self-titled album, commonly referred to as "The Black Albumn", took the world over. Hitting No. 1 in 10 countries, selling 650,000 units in the US in the first week alone. Since the album has been certified platinum 16 times making it the 17th best selling album of all time.
  • Late 1991 - Upon the collapse of the Soviet Union, Metallica performed in the "Monsters of Rock" series introducing Russia to the world of metal. In attendance was over 1.6 million people.
  • 1992 - while touring with Guns N' Roses a the Montreal Olympic Stadium, all hell raised loose. Following a pyrotechnic accident, James Hetfield was rushed to a local hospital leaving the crowd with questions and anticipation on how Guns N' Roses may respond. After a many hour delay, Axl Rose and company join the stage for a couple songs when Axl frustrated left the crowd with no explaination or return to the stage. The crowd left to the streets rioting resulting in flipped cars, burned buildings, and regret.
  • 1996-1997 - Pivoting from their thrash roots, the band releases two albumns (Load, Reload) both debuting at No. 1.
  • 2001 - to the dismay of fans, Jason Newsted leaves the band for personal reasons likely the result of years of hazing, touring, and substance abuse.
  • 2003 - in an effort to produce what would be Bob Rock's final production with Metallica, the band hires ex-Ozzy Osbourne bassist Robert Trujillo to finalize the album "St. Anger". During production, the band was filmed in documentary "Metallica: Some Kind of Monster" which detailed friction between band members, rehab, and revival.
  • 2008 - Getting back to their trash roots the band hires producer Rick Rubin to produce "Death Magnetic" helping the band become the first in history to release five consecutive studio albums a No. 1 on the Billboard 200.
  • 2010 - Seen as a sign of remediation between former guitarist Dave Mustaine (Megadeth) and Metallica founders (James and Lars), the two thrash metal giants joined forces along with Anthrax and Slayer in the "Big Four" tour
  • 2012 - Metallica left Warner Bros. Records to launch their own independent label, Blackened Recordings.
  • 2016 - Metallica releases their 10th studio album "Hardwired... to Self-Destruct". I was lucky to see the first signal "Hardwired" played by the band live at the opening of US Bank Stadium in Minneapolis.
  • 2023 - the band is set to release "72 Seasons" on April 14th to a ravenous fan base couped up at home following a global pandemic.
